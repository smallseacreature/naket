each packet is represented as a stack of protocol layers

Ether is the local network frame, with things like source and destination mac, and ether type (what the next layer is)

IP has source, dest, ttl, protocol, length, fragmentation
UDP has ports, length and checksum

raw means scapy found payload bytes but does not have a loaded protocol definition for them

The other layers mean:

ARP — IP-to-MAC address resolution
TCP — non-BACnet TCP traffic
ICMP — ping and network-control traffic
Dot3 — IEEE 802.3 Ethernet framing
LLC and SNAP — protocols carried inside some 802.3 frames
STP — Spanning Tree Protocol traffic
Padding — extra Ethernet bytes used to meet minimum frame size
